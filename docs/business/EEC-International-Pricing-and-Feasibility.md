# Empire English Community (EEC) — International Pricing & Feasibility
### For members OUTSIDE Egypt (Gulf / GCC, wider Arab world, and Arab diaspora)

> **Assumptions**
> - FX (2026 estimate): **1 USD ≈ 3.67 AED ≈ 50 EGP.**
> - **Founder salary goal: 3,000 AED ≈ $816 ≈ 40,800 EGP / month.**
> - Same zero-cost bootstrap model: free Discord, AI content, you run it, organic growth, reinvest later.
> - International payments via Stripe / PayPal / Paddle (cards work easily) + Gulf local gateways. Fees ~3.5%.
> - Coaching delivered by you at the start = no cash cost, uses your time.
> - Figures are conservative, industry-pattern estimates — not audited data.

## 1. Market Insight (Outside Egypt)

| Segment | Purchasing power | What they pay for | Price expectation |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Gulf / GCC** (UAE, Saudi, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman) | **Very high** | Career English, business, travel, status, results | $30–100/mo feels normal; $200+ for coaching |
| **Levant + North Africa** (Jordan, Lebanon, Iraq, Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia) | **Mixed / mid** | Jobs, migration, university, opportunity | $15–40/mo committed band |

**Key difference vs Egypt:** lower *volume*, but **5–6× higher value per member.** A Gulf or diaspora member happily pays in USD what an Egyptian member pays in EGP numerically (e.g., "39" — but $39 vs 399 EGP). That alone is a ~5× revenue multiplier.

**Competitive gap:** Sits far below premium institutes (Berlitz, Eton, private Gulf tutors at high hourly rates) and per-minute apps (Cambly, italki), while offering a **community + system + accountability** they don't.

## 4. ⭐ How many international members to pay yourself 3,000 AED ($816)?

Because international ARPU is so much higher, the member count drops dramatically.

> **Members needed ≈ $816 ÷ contribution per member.**

| Scenario | VIP | Empire | Recruit + Builder | **Total members for $816** |
|----------|-----|--------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| Cheap-tiers only | 0 | 0 | ~28 | **~28 members** |
| Light premium | 2 | 3 | ~14 | **~19 members** |
| **Balanced (base case)** | 2 | 4 | ~9 | **~15 members** |
| Premium-heavy | 4 | 5 | ~0 | **~9 members** |
| **VIP-led** | 4 | 0 | ~0 | **~4 members** |

### Headline
- **Realistic balanced answer: ~15–20 paying international members** covers your full 3,000 AED salary.
- **Just ~4 VIP clients** ($236 net each) = your entire salary. From four people.
- Compare to Egypt (~90–120 members for the same salary) — **the international market needs ~5–6× fewer members.**

This is the single strongest argument for **prioritizing Gulf + diaspora acquisition** even while Egypt provides volume and social proof.

## 6. Costs & Payments (International)

- **Launch cost: still ~$0.** Same zero-cost bootstrap.
- **Payments:** Stripe / PayPal / Paddle (cards, easy for Gulf + West) + local Gulf options. Higher acceptance than Egypt — fewer failed checkouts.
- **Reinvestment later (from profit):** revenue-share coaches (to scale VIP beyond your time), light targeted ads (Gulf has cheap, high-intent audiences), part-time moderator.
